# About Doc B's Timeline

Every system has constraints sitting dormant — lithography capacity, grid connections, insurance pricing, rare earth processing, permitting timelines. Most aren't binding right now. Then something changes, and a different set wakes up.

**Doc B's Timeline** identifies those moments. Not what happened, but where the pressure went — and who's absorbing it now.

---

## The core question

When a shock enters a system, every actor protects their own stability. Firms protect margins. Utilities protect reserves. Regulators protect legitimacy. Insurers protect solvency. Each adaptation is rational in isolation. But each one pushes the unresolved consequence somewhere else — usually toward whoever has the least leverage to push back.

Each edition asks: *What changed? Which actors adapted, and how? Where did the consequence land? And who's absorbing it now — and why can't they refuse?*

This question works for crises (the strait closed), breakthroughs (the chemistry works, the factory doesn't), and belief shifts (the signal changed what everyone is planning for). The direction of the shock doesn't matter. Where it ends up does.

---

## How articles are built

Each edition is built from structured data — corporate filings, economic indicators, energy figures, practitioner signals — combined with sourced reporting and cross-domain analysis. Claims are sourced and falsifiable. Predictions carry specific dates and stated conditions for being wrong.

The standard: if an article can't explain *why* a constraint exists and *what it forces next*, it doesn't publish.

---

## What we cover

The constraint migration paths that no single-domain reporter can see: critical minerals and supply chains, energy infrastructure, semiconductor manufacturing, defence industrial capacity, geopolitical chokepoints, and what happens when these systems collide.

---

## Predictions

Predictions are embedded in each article with specific dates and explicit kill signals — the conditions under which the thesis would be wrong. A public prediction ledger tracking outcomes is coming.
