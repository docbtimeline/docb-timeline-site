What Doc B's Timeline is and how it works
April 5, 2026 · 2 min · Doc B

About Doc B’s Timeline#

Doc B’s Timeline identifies constraint shifts — the moments when a bottleneck that everyone assumes is permanent moves somewhere unexpected.

The analysis starts from a single question: where is pressure actually building, and what does it do when it can no longer hold?

Each edition maps a specific causal mechanism: not what happened, but why the structure of a system made that outcome likely, and what it forces next. The unit of analysis is the constraint — resource, technological, economic, informational, geopolitical — and the work is tracing what happens when one binding limit loosens or tightens.

The publication covers: critical minerals and supply chains, energy infrastructure, semiconductor manufacturing, defence industrial capacity, geopolitical chokepoints, and the second-order effects when these systems collide.

Methodology#

Every edition is built from 200+ sources per run: structured data feeds (EDGAR corporate filings, FRED economic data, EIA energy figures), practitioner signals from X, Grok-sourced divergence analysis, and primary reporting. Claims are sourced and falsifiable. Predictions carry specific dates and kill signals.

The analytical standard: select on mechanisms, not surface events. If an article can’t explain why a constraint exists and what it forces next, it doesn’t publish.

Track Record#

Predictions are embedded in each article with specific dates and conditions. A public prediction ledger is coming. Until then, the archive is the record.
